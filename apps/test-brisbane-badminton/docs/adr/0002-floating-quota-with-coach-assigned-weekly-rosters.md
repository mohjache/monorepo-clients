# Floating lesson quota with coach-assigned weekly rosters

An Enrollment grants a Student a per-Term **Lesson quota** (from their Plan) rather than binding them to fixed weekly Classes. Each week, the **Head Coach** builds the Session rosters by assigning enrolled Students to specific Sessions, constrained by eligibility (audience/level), Student and Coach availability, and the target coaching ratio. Assignments can change week to week as coach and slot availability change.

This reflects how the academy already runs: the head coach holds rough numbers and pre-assigns slots, adjusting weekly. We rejected both fixed-slot enrollment (too rigid for their real scheduling) and self-serve student booking (students don't choose their own slot; the coach places them).

Consequence: capacity optimisation is primarily a **head-coach-facing planning aid** — surfacing per-Session ratio, crowding, and emptiness as the roster is drafted — plus notifications to Students about their assigned Session each week. It is not a student-facing booking-cap gate.

Supersedes the fixed-weekly-slot model considered while drafting the glossary.
